10-26-2016

After three months, I earned my yellow belt. This was the first test I had to take to earn a belt—I broke my first ever wooden board with a front kick.





To earn my purple belt, I had to learn more complex techniques. This was the first belt that moved me to a higher-rank class.

07-26-2017





01-24-2018

During my test for blue belt, I had to break a board with a spin roundhouse kick. I missed the board during my first attempt for the first time in all my tests. However, I was able to do it my second try. Although it was a little bit embarrassing, it was an important failure. I became more humble and trained harder than ever before.





Earning my dark blue belt resulted in moving up to a higher rank class. This was a level of skill that I was completely unused to, but I worked hard to catch up to the others.

07-19-2018





01-20-2019

The test for recommended black belt was a completely different level than all the others. I was aware that it was notorious for being difficult, though, so I had practiced for weeks before. I was able to do everything successfully the first time and pass the test.





Years of effort and time finally culminated into the black belt test. This was the longest and most difficult test of all—I had to demonstrate all the techniques I had learned throughout the past five years, break wooden boards, and spar multiple rounds. I was able to pass my first try, though, and earn by black belt during my freshman year.
